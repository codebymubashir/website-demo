Our Story

Built around one fire.

Eleven years ago, a single wood-fired grill and a family recipe book turned into Ember & Oak. Here's how we got here — and why we still do things the slow way.

Since 2014

From a backyard grill to a full house

Ember & Oak started with Chacha Rasheed's backyard grill and a habit of feeding half the street on Sundays. What began as family dinners turned into a standing invitation, and eventually into these four walls.

The name comes from the two things that never leave our kitchen: the ember that starts every fire, and the oak that keeps it burning slow. We still light that fire by hand, every single morning, before the first guest walks in.

Today the same family still runs the floor, the same grill still faces the dining room, and the food still tastes like it did on those Sunday afternoons — just with a proper kitchen behind it.
